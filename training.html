How to run this block

Schedule. Mon AM optional pump (squash PM) · Tue off gym (BV PM) · Wed AM upper push (BV PM) · Thu AM lower-eccentric · Fri AM upper pull · Sat lower-heavy (later start is fine) · Sun off.

Why Saturday legs works. The heavy day sits in the freshest slot of the week — rested, awake, 55 h before Monday squash and 79 h before Tuesday BV. The DOMS-maker day (Thu) has its aftermath land on Fri/Sat, fully sport-free. No Saturday this week? Run the heavy session Monday morning instead — its concentric-bias design is squash-safe. That's the fallback, not a failure.

The optional Monday is the release valve. Five gym days + three sports + daily cycling is the ceiling. Feeling heavy, knee grumpy, or short on sleep? Drop Monday first, guilt-free — the four fixed days are the complete program.

Progression (double progression). Stay at a load until you hit the top of the rep range on all sets at the target RIR, then add: +2.5–5 kg lower body, +1–2.5 kg upper. Pull-ups: reps first, then weight.

Right side first. Lead every single-leg/arm move with the right; match the left. Right feels weaker? One extra set on the right — never the left.

Knee. The Thursday wall sits + tempo work are treatment, not threat — tendons like regular, controlled load. Skip the lateral bound any day the knee is a 2+.

Leg-day warm-up. The 10-move stretch flow (30–45 s each), tendon prep on Friday, then ramp-up sets: ~50% and ~75% of work weight on the first lift, one light set before each later lift. Short on time: cat-cow, downward dog, world's greatest stretch, figure four, supine twists.

Deload when the app suggests it (knee flaring, or 5+ weeks hard): keep loads, cut every exercise to 2 sets, skip throws and bounds for the week.

RIR = reps in reserve. RIR 2 ≈ stop 2 shy of failure. Log your top set only.
